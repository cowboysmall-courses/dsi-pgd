ML Case Study - Hospital Readmission Prediction¶

Objective:¶

Investigate and identify key factors contributing to hospital readmissions, which occur when patients are readmitted to the hospital shortly after discharge. By analyzing these factors, the goal is to develop predictive models that can assist in anticipating which patients are more likely to experience readmission.

Data Description¶

Field Description
age Age bracket of the patient
time_in_hospital Days (from 1 to 14)
n_procedures Number of procedures performed during the hospital stay
n_lab_procedures Number of laboratory procedures during the hospital stay
n_medications Number of medications administered during the hospital stay
n_outpatient Number of outpatient visits in the year before a hospital stay
n_inpatient Number of inpatient visits in the year before the hospital stay
n_emergency Number of visits to the emergency room in the year before the hospital stay
diagnosis Primary diagnosis (Circulatory, Respiratory, Digestive, etc.)
glucose_test Whether the glucose serum came out as high (> 200), normal, or not performed
A1Ctest Whether the A1C level of the patient came out as high (> 7%), normal, or not performed
change Whether there was a change in the diabetes medication ('yes' or 'no')
diabetes_med Whether a diabetes medication was prescribed ('yes' or 'no')
readmitted If the patient was readmitted at the hospital ('yes' or 'no')

Model 1. Binary Logistic Regression¶

Splitting Data into Train and Test data sets

In [7]:
x_variables =['age', 'time_in_hospital', 'n_lab_procedures', 'n_procedures',
       'n_medications', 'n_outpatient', 'n_inpatient', 'n_emergency',
       'diagnosis', 'glucose_test', 'A1Ctest', 'change', 'diabetes_med']

X = data.loc[:,x_variables]
y = data.loc[:, 'readmitted']

#Creating dummies for categorical variables (required for ML algorithms)
X = pd.get_dummies(X, columns=['age','diagnosis', 'glucose_test', 'A1Ctest', 'change', 'diabetes_med'], drop_first=True)

#Splitting data into train and test
X_train, X_test, y_train, y_test = train_test_split(X, y, 
                                                   test_size=0.30, 
                                                   random_state = 0)

Model 2. Naive Bayes¶

The scikit-learn library has two functions for Naive Bayes method:

  1. GaussianNB() for numeric predictors
  2. MultinomialNB() for categorical predictors

Here, we use GaussianNB() by converting categorical variables into numeric variables(dummy variables). This is a general industry practice. However one can convert numeric variable into categorical variable using MultinomialNB()

For example :

Convert 'time_in_hospital' to quartile categories

data['time_in_hospital'] = pd.qcut(data['time_in_hospital'], q=4, labels=['Q1', 'Q2', 'Q3', 'Q4'])
